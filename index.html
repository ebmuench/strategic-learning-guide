Learning = Change in Long-Term Memory

Not completion rates‚Äîactual behavior change.

For Leaders: This is your ROI benchmark. Research defines learning as a change in long-term memory that enables sustained application:

  • Performance metrics (completion, quiz scores) show short-term recall
  • True learning shows up in changed behavior on the job weeks later

Ask: "Are we measuring what people did in training, or what they're doing differently at work?"

Why Information Overload Fails

The science behind wasted training time

For Leaders: Working memory (where thinking happens) holds only 4-7 items. This explains why:

  • Information-dump training doesn't stick‚Äîit overwhelms
  • Practice and application are essential, not optional extras
  • Shorter, focused experiences outperform marathon sessions

Red flag: Learning content that "covers everything" but includes no practice.

The Wasted Effort Problem

Cognitive load and organizational efficiency

For Leaders: Not all mental effort produces learning. Watch for:

  • Wasted effort: Confusing navigation, irrelevant content, poor design forcing extra work
  • Productive struggle: Challenging practice on relevant skills‚Äîthis IS learning

Ask vendors: "How have you minimized cognitive load from poor design to maximize time on relevant practice?"

The 9 Elements to Evaluate Learning Investments

Clear Goals: Tied to specific organizational outcomes‚Äîemployees know exactly what capability they're building and why it matters
Balanced Challenge: Right difficulty level‚Äînot so easy it's boring, not so hard it's frustrating (the "flow zone")
Contextualized: Practice in contexts that match real work situations‚Äîensuring skills transfer to the job
Anchored: Activities that employees recognize as useful applications of their work‚Äînot abstract exercises
Relevant: Connected to employee goals and career growth‚Äîthey care about succeeding
Exploratory: Safe space to try, fail, and learn‚Äîpsychological safety that encourages risk-taking
Direct Manipulation: Practice that looks like real performance‚Äînot just reading or watching
Appropriate Feedback: Consequences that mirror real results‚Äîideally natural outcomes, not just "correct/incorrect"
Attention-Maintaining: Variety and surprises that maintain focus‚Äînot predictable click-through monotony
Strategic Step 1

Hook 'Em: Win Employee Buy-In

The difference between compliance and engagement starts before the learning even begins.

"To get people ready to engage, you have to open them up emotionally before you can address them cognitively. Otherwise, their minds may as well be coated with Teflon."

For Leaders: This is why mandating training doesn't produce results. You can require attendance, but you can't mandate learning.

The Three Beliefs Your Workforce Needs:

1

"I need to know this"

Employees must see personal relevance and real consequences‚Äînot just organizational mandates.

Leader Action:

  • Connect learning to their goals, not just company goals
  • Show real business consequences (lost deals, customer complaints, safety incidents)
  • Make the "WIIFM" explicit: career growth, efficiency, reduced stress
  • Use data and stories together‚Äîaggregate impact plus visceral examples

Leader Example: Before a compliance training, share a real incident‚Äîwhat happened, the cost, the human impact. "This is why we're investing in this."

2

"I don't know this already"

Break overconfidence. Experienced employees often think they don't need training.

Leader Action:

  • Demand learning that starts with a realistic challenge that reveals gaps
  • Support the discomfort of discovering you don't know something
  • Share your own learning moments‚Äîmodel that growth is ongoing

Leader Example: "Our top performers scored 60% on the pre-assessment. This isn't about checking a box‚Äîthere's real value here."

3

"This experience will change that"

Build trust that the time invested will actually produce results‚Äînot just seat time.

Leader Action:

  • Vet learning experiences before rolling them out‚Äîdemand quality
  • Acknowledge if past training has been poor: "I know we've had some duds. This is different because..."
  • Set expectations for how learning will be supported and applied
  • Make time and space for learning‚Äîdon't pile it on top of impossible workloads
Self-Determination Theory: What drives your employees' intrinsic motivation?

Practice That Transfers to the Job

For Leaders: Practice is where learning investment either pays off or gets wasted. Evaluate using the Goal : Role : World framework:

üéØ Goal: Does the practice match real job challenges? (Not just "answer questions about the content")
üë§ Role: Are learners practicing as themselves in their actual role? (Not passive observers)
üåç World: Does the context match where they'll apply the skill? (Realistic scenarios = better transfer)

Red Flags for Leaders:

  • Practice is just "knowledge check" quizzes
  • Scenarios feel generic or unrealistic to your industry
  • No opportunity to make mistakes and learn from them
  • All practice is passive (watching, reading) not active (doing, deciding)

Feedback That Drives Behavior Change

‚ùå Low-ROI approach: "The correct answer is B because..." (tells, doesn't teach)
‚úì High-ROI approach: Show consequences of choices first‚Äîlet the outcome teach the lesson

What Leaders Should Look For:

  • Does feedback show realistic consequences? (Customer leaves, deal fails, safety incident)
  • Do employees see why choices matter‚Äînot just that they were "wrong"?
  • Is there opportunity to try again with new understanding?
  • Does feedback connect to the mental models that guide good decisions?

Strategic Step 3

Evaluating & Commissioning Quality Learning

What leaders should demand from internal teams, vendors, and partners.

The Strategic Design Process

For Leaders: You don't need to be a learning designer, but you should know what good design looks like. Use this to evaluate proposals, ask better questions, and hold teams accountable for outcomes‚Äînot just deliverables.

What to Demand at Each Stage:

1

Analysis: Is Learning the Right Solution?

Before investing, verify that learning will actually solve the business problem.

Questions Leaders Should Ask:

  • What specific performance gap are we closing? How do we know it exists?
  • Is this a skill gap or something else (incentives, tools, process, culture)?
  • What will success look like? How will we measure behavior change?
  • What's the business impact if we succeed? If we don't?

Leader Warning: If the real problem is poor processes, bad incentives, or inadequate tools‚Äîtraining won't fix it. Don't waste the investment.

2

Requirements: What Makes This Learning Strategic?

Ensure learning is designed around real job performance, not content coverage.

Questions Leaders Should Ask:

  • What will people DO differently after this? (Not just know)
  • What decisions do they need to make? What mistakes do people typically make?
  • How does this connect to org strategy and employee career paths?
  • What will make employees WANT to engage with this?

Involve Your People:

  • High performers: What separates great from average?
  • Target audience: What would make this valuable to you?
3

Review: Is This Actually Good Learning?

Evaluate designs and pilots against the 9 elements.

Before Approving, Verify:

  1. Does it start with a compelling hook that creates buy-in?
  2. Is practice realistic and job-relevant‚Äînot just knowledge checks?
  3. Does feedback show consequences, not just "correct/incorrect"?
  4. Is challenge calibrated to the audience?
  5. Would YOU want to spend time on this?
4

Measure: Did It Actually Work?

Track real outcomes, not just completion.

Metrics That Matter:

  • Level 1: Did employees find it valuable? (Not just "liked it")
  • Level 2: Can they demonstrate the skill? (Performance, not quiz scores)
  • Level 3: Are they applying it on the job? (Manager observations, metrics)
  • Level 4: Is it impacting business outcomes?

Strategic Move: Build measurement into the design upfront. Know how you'll evaluate success before you launch.
